# Refund Knowledge Base

## What this team can confirm
- Refund requests are reviewed case-by-case.
- Every refund request gets a human reply — never auto-approved, never auto-denied.
- We aim to respond to refund requests within 1 business day.
- Refund eligibility depends on plan terms.

## What we NEVER promise in a reply
- That a refund will be approved.
- A specific refund amount.
- A specific refund timeline.
- Anything binding about eligibility.

## Safe phrasing
- "Thanks for reaching out. I've logged your refund request and routed it to our Billing team. They'll review and reply with next steps within one business day."
- "Refund reviews happen on a case-by-case basis. A team member will reach out with details."
- "I'm sorry to hear the product didn't meet your expectations. Your request has been escalated."

## Common intents
1. Refund after subscription cancel → confirm receipt, escalate.
2. Double charge refund → route via Billing + Refund paths.
3. Dissatisfaction refund → apologize, escalate, never pre-judge the outcome.
4. Refund for usage they didn't make → capture details, escalate.

## Hard rule
If the draft ever contains a refund amount, timeline, or approval language, the guardrail MUST block it.
